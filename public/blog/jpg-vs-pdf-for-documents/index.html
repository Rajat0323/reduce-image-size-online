JPG vs PDF for Documents: Which Format Should You Use?

JPG and PDF are both useful, but they solve different problems. JPG is an image format. PDF is a document format. Choosing the right one depends on whether you need a photo or a structured file.

Use JPG for Single Images

JPG is ideal for photos, screenshots, and quick image sharing. It is compact, widely supported, and easy to open on almost every device.

Use PDF for Documents

PDF is better when the file needs to behave like a document. It can hold multiple pages, preserve order, and print more predictably.

School and Business Uploads

Many schools, job portals, and business systems prefer PDF because it is easier to review and store. A single PDF also prevents missing pages.

Printing and Archives

PDF gives you a cleaner printing workflow and a better long-term archive format for scanned documents, forms, reports, and certificates.
