## How to choose the right target file size

There is no single "correct" file size — it depends on where the image will live. For amazon sellers, shopify merchants, and product photographers, I use a simple rule of thumb: match the destination, then leave a small safety margin. A profile avatar rarely needs more than 100KB, a feed photo usually looks great between 150KB and 300KB, and a full-width hero image can sit around 200KB to 400KB after resizing. When a portal publishes a hard cap, aim 5–10% under it so metadata and re-encoding never push you back over the line at the last second.

If you are unsure where to start, open the general [Image Compressor](https://www.reduceimagesizeonline.com/image-compressor) and watch the live output size as you move the quality slider. When you need an exact number rather than a range, switch to a preset such as [Compress to 100KB](https://www.reduceimagesizeonline.com/compress-image-to-100kb) or [Compress to 200KB](https://www.reduceimagesizeonline.com/compress-image-to-200kb) instead of guessing and re-exporting several times.

## A quick before-and-after quality check

Before submitting anything, I run the same three checks every time. First, zoom to 100% and inspect faces, logos, and small text — these are where over-compression shows first. Second, confirm the pixel dimensions match what the platform asked for, not just the KB size, because a small file at the wrong dimensions still fails.
